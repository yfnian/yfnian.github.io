Yefan Nian

Ph.D. candidate

Food and Resource Economics Department

University of Florida

Welcome! I am an agricultural economist studying agribusiness. My current research agenda has been broadly focused on using behavioral economic theories and experimental economic and applied econometric methods to analyze interconnections among the food system, food policy, and human decision-making related to agriculture sustainability issues. My research covers both consumption and production sides of the agricultural system. My research has been published on Ecological Economics, Water Resources Research, and Applied Economics.

I have also been fortunate to participate in a few multidisciplinary research teams to conduct extension works. Specifically, I develop economic decision support tools and analyze the economic feasibility of new sustainable agricultural production technologies and practices. My work has resulted in peer-reviewed journal articles on HortTechnology and a few university’s extension publications. [Download my CV]
